Zhare and the Philippine leader arrived at the hotel, where they met with Britain to finalize the details of their planned mock battle. The discussion proceeded as follows:

  1. Participants – The battle would involve both nations: Britain and Zhare.
  2. Weaponry – Only medieval weaponry would be allowed at all times.
  3. Restrictions – No guns, no ammunition, no modern weaponry of any kind.
  4. Battlefield Location – Britain had communicated with Canada to use some designated areas within Canada as the battlefield.
  5. Logistics – All transportation costs would be covered by Britain.
  6. Spoils of War – There would be rewards for the victor.
  7. Winning, Losing, and Stalemate Criteria:
    • Winning Condition – If 10% of the allocated army is "down," that nation wins.
    • Losing Condition – If 90% of a nation’s army is "down," they are defeated.
    • Stalemate Condition – If both armies sustain equal losses.
  8. Troop Allocation – A total of approximately 2 5,000 soldiers would participate in the battle.

These arrangements were settled over the course of about four days.
